Minimal Final Statement

The full profile-sieve EL argument can be compressed into one geometric statement:
In L2 (PT ), the model is y = β0 x + µ0 + u. A candidate partition and sieve
define a nuisance subspace NK,Λ,T . Profiling is orthogonal projection onto this
subspace, and both the outcome residual and score weight are replaced by their
⊥T
components in NK,Λ,T
. The profiled moment is the inner product
ΨT (β, Λ) = ⟨MK,Λ (y − βx), MK,Λ w⟩T .
At the true nuisance space, the nuisance component vanishes under M , leaving
only projected noise plus approximation error. A wrong nuisance subspace leaves
a projection drift. EL is then a scalar likelihood for the projected score array. As
T → ∞, the empirical Hilbert geometry converges to the population geometry
in L2 (P ), where
Ψ(β, Λ0 ) = ⟨MΛP0 (Y − βX), MΛP0 w⟩P .
