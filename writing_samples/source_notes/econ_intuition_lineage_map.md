# Economic Intuition Lineage Map

## One-Sentence Positioning

Perron showed that broken trends can be mistaken for unit roots; the finance predictability literature shows that persistent predictors and unstable return regressions create fragile evidence; this paper shows that when the broken object is an unknown nonlinear nuisance component, profile-sieve empirical likelihood can recover inference for a stable predictive slope.

## Branch 1: Spurious Regression And Persistence

Granger-Newbold and Phillips are the prehistory. Their core lesson is that persistent or integrated time series can create apparently significant regressions even when the economic relation is absent.

Use this branch to motivate the general danger:

```text
apparent signal can be generated by time-series geometry rather than structural predictability.
```

The distinction is that their mechanism is stochastic persistence or stochastic trends, while this paper's mechanism is omitted broken nonlinear nuisance structure.

## Branch 2: Unit Root Versus Broken Trend

Nelson-Plosser made unit-root evidence in macroeconomic series canonical. Perron then showed that allowing major breaks, such as the Great Crash and the oil-price shock, could reverse the interpretation for many series.

This is the deepest ancestor of the paper:

```text
Perron: broken deterministic trend can look like a unit root.
This paper: broken nonlinear nuisance can look like stable predictive content.
```

Christiano, Zivot-Andrews, Banerjee-Lumsdaine-Stock, Lumsdaine-Papell, Kim-Perron, and Perron-Yabu supply the warnings that break dates cannot simply be hand-picked and that nuisance breaks must be treated symmetrically under the null and alternative.

## Branch 3: Unknown And Multiple Break Engines

Andrews and Bai-Perron provide the econometric engine for unknown structural change. Bai-Perron is useful here as a segmentation ancestor, but it is not the paper's novelty.

The paper's break search is different because it works inside a profile-sieve structure:

```text
fixed-dimensional segmented regression
becomes
growing-K block-sieve nuisance segmentation with empirical-likelihood inference for beta.
```

The important theory bridge is that the estimated and true nuisance partitions give first-order equivalent score behavior under the localization and bridge conditions.

## Branch 4: Breaks Can Mimic Persistence

The regime-switching and occasional-break literature shows that discrete shifts can mimic long memory, unit-root-like behavior, or high persistence.

This branch supports the phrase "false persistence." It helps explain why the paper is not just about detecting breaks, but about identifying a mechanism through which breaks contaminate the slope evidence.

## Branch 5: Predictive Regressions With Persistent Predictors

Stambaugh, Cavanagh-Elliott-Stock, and Campbell-Yogo are the key persistent-predictor inference references. Their message is that predictive regressions with persistent regressors and correlated innovations have nonstandard finite-sample and asymptotic behavior.

The paper should not present these methods as wrong. It should say:

```text
persistent-predictor corrections address one source of fragile inference;
they do not, by themselves, remove omitted broken nonlinear nuisance components.
```

## Branch 6: Return Predictability Instability

Fama-French and Campbell-Shiller are the classic return-predictability foundation. Goyal-Welch is the benchmark dataset and empirical challenge: many predictors look unstable or weak, especially out of sample.

Lettau-Van Nieuwerburgh and Paye-Timmermann are close finance ancestors because they emphasize shifts and instability in valuation ratios or predictive relations.

The natural empirical battlefield is therefore:

```text
Goyal-Welch predictors
+ persistence diagnostics, with published persistent-predictor corrections only if implemented separately
+ linear break adjustment
+ no-break nonlinear sieve
+ break-aware nonlinear nuisance profile EL.
```

## Where The Paper Sits

The paper should be positioned as the intersection of three ideas:

```text
apparent persistence can be false;
breaks must be estimated rather than assumed;
the broken object can be a nonlinear nuisance, not the structural slope.
```

The key sentence:

```text
Broken nonlinear nuisance components can masquerade as stable predictive slope evidence.
```

This framing lets the empirical section stand beside Perron, Bai-Perron, Campbell-Yogo, Goyal-Welch, Lettau-Van Nieuwerburgh, and Paye-Timmermann without claiming that any one of those literatures is simply mistaken.
